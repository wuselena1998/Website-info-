New York

New York has relatively low levels of background radiation, primarily from natural sources and building materials. Occasional concerns arise from medical facilities and minor industrial uses, but public safety regulations keep exposure minimal.

Tokyo

Tokyo maintains low radiation levels since the Fukushima disaster, with extensive monitoring to ensure food, water, and environmental safety. Public areas are safe for residents and visitors, with international verification of radiation levels.

Chernobyl

Chernobyl remains one of the most radioactive sites in the world after the 1986 disaster. While some areas are open for limited tourism under strict controls, the Exclusion Zone still has regions where radiation is dangerously high and uninhabitable.
