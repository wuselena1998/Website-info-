What is Nuclear Radiation?

Nuclear radiation refers to the particles and energy released during radioactive decay. It includes alpha particles, beta particles, gamma rays, and more. While natural radiation exists in our environment, excessive exposure can pose health risks.

Common Sources of Radiation

  • Medical Imaging (X-rays, CT scans)
  • Nuclear Power Plants
  • Cosmic Rays from Space
  • Natural Radioactive Materials (like Radon gas)

Radiation Safety

To stay safe around radiation, it's important to limit exposure time, increase distance from the source, and use appropriate shielding.
