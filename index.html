Radiation Awareness

What is Radiation?

Radiation is energy that travels through space. Some types of radiation can be seen, like visible light, while others are invisible, like X-rays and gamma rays.

Types of Radiation

  • Alpha Particles: Heavy, positively charged particles that can be stopped by paper or skin.
  • Beta Particles: Lighter, negatively charged particles that can penetrate further but are blocked by materials like plastic or glass.
  • Gamma Rays: Highly penetrating electromagnetic radiation requiring dense materials like lead for shielding.

Sources of Radiation

Natural sources include the sun, soil, rocks, and even food. Man-made sources include medical imaging (like X-rays), nuclear reactors, and industrial equipment.

Radiation Safety

Radiation exposure can be minimized by using three main principles: time, distance, and shielding.

  • Limit the time spent near a radiation source.
  • Increase distance from the radiation source.
  • Use appropriate shielding to block or reduce radiation exposure.

Why Learn About Radiation?

Understanding radiation helps promote safety and smart decision-making, especially for people working in healthcare, nuclear energy, and research industries.
